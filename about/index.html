My Work

I am an R&D engineer working in magnetic resonance imaging (MRI). I draw upon my experience and training in physics, NMR
spectroscopy and MRI imaging to acquire, transfer, archive, and analyze data to derive insights about health and physiology
from imaging data. I develop, implement and test methods to image vascular physiology, brain activity and structure.
As part of a team of research assistants and engineers working at an imaging center, I provide technical oversight, physics
and computer science skills to projects including protocol optimization, development and testing of data processing
pipelines, assuring data quality and improving efficiency of methods and services.

In my research and development role, I apply quantitative physical models to acquire data optimally and write computer
programs in C++, Linux, python, MATLAB and other languages that enable MRI scanners to acquire the data that encodes
useful information to answer research questions, and to transfer, store and process the data. This is interesting and
varied work that has exposed me to hardware programming, image processing, modeling and analysis as well as methods to
transfer, store and perform quality assurance tests on large data sets. On occasion, I build electronics that improve the
sensitivity of existing hardware, and design and fabricate 3D printed fixtures and housings to position samples within
that hardware for robust and optimal function.

Working in a data engineering role, I design, specify and manage the deployment of data systems located on premises, at
data centers and using cloud services. Working with systems administrators, hardware vendors and cloud service providers,
I help specify and design these multi-server systems and deploy data pipelines on them that are used by many users of an
imaging center.
